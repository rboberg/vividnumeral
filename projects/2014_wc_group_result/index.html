Is That Sound You Hear Goliath Falling?

At the start of the World Cup, I published predictions [LINK] of the outcome of the group stage, giving each team a probability of advancing. A day later Nate Silver at FiveThirtyEight.com [LINK] published his own predictions. Thus began an epic battle of statistical forecasters[LINK]: David (VividNumeral, me writing out my basement garage apartment) versus Goliath (FiveThirtyEight, a subsidiary of ESPN).

Well, the group stage is over and the results are in. The chart on the left tells you everything you need to know - FiveThirtyEight made bigger prediction errors on average than VividNumeral. I can officially declare a resounding victory for VividNumeral.

Goliath has fallen.

Thump.

Or is That the Sound a Shrug Makes?

Now that I've satisfied my ego, it's time to say outloud what you, my quant savy reader, probably already figured out. The chart that "tells you everything you need to know" tells you almost nothing you need to know.

My average prediction error was indeed smaller than FiveThirtyEight, but with a relevant scale on the chart the difference is imperceptable.
